Cuilvor vs Function Health: the lab and the coach

Function Health's product is testing - 100+ biomarkers a year for a flat fee, in the US. That's a genuinely great input. Cuilvor's product is the reading: it ingests biomarkers from anywhere (Function included, but also the NHS, Superpower, Everlywell, your local hospital - via PDF or even a screenshot), trends them across every panel you've ever taken, and folds them into a weekly coaching review alongside your sleep and food.

UK angle worth knowing: Function doesn't operate here. Cuilvor is lab-agnostic by design - NHS results work day one.

Frequently asked

Is Cuilvor a blood testing service?

No - Cuilvor doesn't draw blood. It reads results from whatever service or hospital you use and turns them into trends and plain-English flags for your GP.

Can NHS blood results be used?

Yes - screenshots from the NHS app work, since the NHS often doesn't allow PDF downloads. Every biomarker is extracted, flagged against the report's own ranges, and trended.

Does Cuilvor interpret out-of-range results?

It explains what a marker does and flags what's worth a GP conversation. It never diagnoses - that line is deliberate and hard-coded into the coaching rules.

Can I use Function Health AND Cuilvor?

That's arguably the ideal stack in the US: Function draws and measures, Cuilvor reads, trends and coaches alongside your lifestyle data.
