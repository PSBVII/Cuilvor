06 · Honest limits

What's stopping it tracking everything?

Less than you'd think. Three real constraints:

🔌

Data you can't reach

A few apps lock their data (no API, no export). Everything else — API, MCP, PDF, CSV, or a sentence from you — gets in.

✍️

Logging friction

The coach only knows what arrives. Wearables remove friction for sleep/training; food still needs a 5-second sentence unless you connect Alma.

🧪

Estimation, not magic

Macro estimates for unlabelled food are ~±15%. Branded items (that UK cheeseburger, that Grenade bar) are near-exact via published nutrition data. Precision follows effort.

Workout tracking and programming? Already in scope — log sessions in chat (or connect Strava) and the weekly review adjusts training suggestions against your recovery and zone-2 target. And one boundary by design: it flags what a doctor should see; it never plays doctor.

07 · Start

Your first week

  1. Log today's food and sleep in one sentence to Claude. That's the whole habit.
  2. Drop your most recent blood test PDF into a chat. Instant baseline, even if it's old.
  3. Tell Claude your goals + injuries so the protocol knows you. "Update my longevity protocol: …"
  4. Click "Run now" on the weekly-longevity-review scheduled task once. Pre-approves its access so Sundays run unattended.
  5. When ready: connect Strava & Alma (one click each) — then a wearable. Each one deletes a logging chore forever.
